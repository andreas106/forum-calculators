About the Streak Calculator

A streak bet (also called a "win in a row" prop or a "perfect-N" parlay-equivalent) pays out if you win some number of consecutive bets within a defined series. Sportsbooks offer them as marketing products — "win 5 picks in a row this week and we'll boost your payout" — and bettors also use streak math privately to size their bankroll against the inevitable losing runs.

This calculator answers two related questions. First, the simple one: if every bet wins with probability p, what's the chance of winning N in a row straight away? That's just pN. Second, the more useful one: across M total bets, what's the chance of getting at least one streak of N consecutive wins anywhere in the series? That's harder — it depends on the full sequence — and we compute it via a Markov-chain walk over the per-bet state.

Streak bets favour the house

A typical sportsbook streak promo offers a fixed payout multiplier for hitting N in a row. The fair multiplier — what a no-vig book would pay — is 1 / pN. Books typically pay 50-70% of that. At −110 per bet (52.38% win), the fair multiplier for 5 in a row is ~25× the stake, but the promo might pay 15× — that's roughly 40% hold on the streak ticket. Streak bets are entertainment products, not value bets. Treat them as such.

The bigger insight from the streak math is for bankroll planning. A break-even bettor at 50% with a 1% edge will still hit losing streaks of 7 or 8 bets several times across a 100-bet season. Sizing your unit so a 10-bet losing streak doesn't wipe you out is more important than picking the right bet — most professional bettors stake 1-3% of bankroll per wager for exactly this reason.

Independent bets only

The math assumes each bet is independent with the same per-bet win probability. That's a reasonable approximation when you're betting on different games or different sports, but breaks down if your bets are correlated (e.g. all on the same team, or all on overs in similar weather conditions). Correlated streaks are harder to model and usually shorter than independent streaks at the same per-bet win rate.

Odds format reference

American (−110)
Negative number = stake to win 100. Standard at US sportsbooks. The implied % is what feeds the streak math.
Decimal (1.91)
Total return per 1 unit staked. Implied % = 1 / decimal.
Fractional (10/11)
Net profit per unit staked. Common in horse racing.
Hong Kong / Indonesian / Malay
Asian formats — all map back to the same per-bet implied probability.
Implied probability (52.38%)
The direct input the streak math uses. Bypass odds conversion by entering this directly if you already know your win rate from a model.
