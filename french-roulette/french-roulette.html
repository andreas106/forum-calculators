About the French Roulette Calculator

French roulette is the original European-rules variant: a 37-pocket wheel with a single zero, identical layout to standard European roulette, plus one of two rules that protect even-money bets when the ball lands on zero. Those two rules — La Partage and En Prison — give French roulette the lowest house edge of any standard roulette variant, and one of the lowest in the entire casino on the affected bets.

This calculator computes the house edge, win probability, payout, and expected loss for any standard bet on a French wheel. Pick the bet type, choose the zero rule your table is running, and enter your stake. The "Expected loss per 100 spins" line is the most practical output — it tells you the average bankroll bleed if you sit down and spin 100 times.

Why French roulette has the lowest house edge in the casino

On a French wheel with La Partage or En Prison, the house edge on even-money bets is 1.35% — lower than blackjack with standard rules (typically 0.5%–1.5% depending on rule set), lower than baccarat banker (1.06%), and dramatically lower than American roulette's 5.26%. If you're going to play roulette, French rules on red/black is the mathematically best bet on the floor. Most other bets on the French wheel still carry the same 2.70% edge as European roulette — the special rules only protect even-money outside bets.

The two rules are mathematically equivalent. La Partage returns half your stake immediately when the ball lands on zero. En Prison locks your bet in place for one more spin — if your bet wins on that next spin, you get your full original stake back (no profit). Both produce the same expected value: half the loss you'd take under standard European rules.

Column — 2:1
