About the Baccarat Calculator

Baccarat is the simplest table game in the casino: two hands are dealt — Player and Banker — and you bet on which will end closer to a total of 9. The drawing rules are fixed (no decisions to make), so there's no strategy beyond which bet to place. The whole game reduces to picking among a small set of bets with known house edges. Banker is the best, Player is a close second, and Tie is one of the worst bets in the casino.

This calculator gives you the exact probabilities, payout, house edge, and expected loss for any standard bet on an 8-deck shoe. Pair side bets (Player Pair / Banker Pair) are included for completeness — they're available on most tables but carry significantly worse odds than the main wagers.

Why Banker is the best bet on the table

Banker wins 45.86% of resolved hands versus Player's 44.62% — a real edge baked into the drawing rules. The 5% commission on Banker wins exists precisely to claw back that advantage; without it, Banker would be a player-favourable bet. After the commission, the house keeps a 1.06% edge on Banker versus 1.24% on Player. The takeaway: when in doubt, bet Banker. The "always bet Banker" strategy isn't a system — it's just the mathematically correct default given the bet menu.

The Tie bet is the famous trap. At 8:1 it carries a 14.36% house edge — more than 10× the edge on Banker or Player. The 9:1 version (offered at some Asian and online casinos) drops the edge to 4.84%, which is still worse than red/black on American roulette. There's no version of the Tie bet that competes with the main wagers, and the "ties happen in clusters" pattern bettors think they see is selection bias, not signal.

Side bets: usually skip

Pair bets (Player Pair, Banker Pair) pay 11:1 if the first two cards dealt to that side form a pair (e.g. two 7s). The probability is roughly 7.47% on an 8-deck shoe, producing a 10.36% house edge. Some casinos offer additional side bets — Perfect Pair, Either Pair, Big/Small, Dragon 7, Panda 8 — most of which carry edges in the 4-15% range. They make the game more interesting but cost you bankroll if played routinely.
